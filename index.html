About Me

In the depths of time and memory, I am known by many names. To some, I am Mithrandir, the Grey Pilgrim. To others, I am Gandalf the Grey, a wanderer of Middle-earth. But in truth, I am Olórin, one of the Maiar, sent by the Valar to aid the Free Peoples of Middle-earth in their darkest hour.

My journey began in the distant West, where I dwelt in the gardens of Irmo, the Vala of Dreams. There, I learned much of the world and its ways, and I was schooled in the arts of wisdom and counsel. But when the Shadow began to stir in the East, I knew that my place was not among the gardens, but in the world of mortals.

So it was that I came to Middle-earth, clad in grey and bearing a staff, to guide and protect those who would stand against the growing darkness. I roamed the lands, from the misty forests of Mirkwood to the towering peaks of the Misty Mountains, always seeking out those who needed my aid.

In my travels, I met many brave souls, each with their own part to play in the unfolding drama of Middle-earth. I stood beside Aragorn, the heir of Isildur, and watched as he grew from a ranger of the North into a king of men. I counseled Théoden, the king of Rohan, and helped him find the strength to stand against the forces of Saruman.

But perhaps my greatest role was in the quest of Frodo Baggins, a simple hobbit from the Shire. It was I who first suspected the true nature of the ring he bore, and I who urged him to take it to Mount Doom, where it could be destroyed. It was a perilous journey, filled with danger and despair, but together, we faced it and emerged victorious.

Now, as the age draws to a close and the world changes around me, I remain ever vigilant, watching and waiting for the next chapter in the tale of Middle-earth to unfold. My name may be forgotten, my deeds may fade into legend, but while there is still light in the world, I will continue to stand against the darkness, for that is my purpose, my destiny, and my joy.
